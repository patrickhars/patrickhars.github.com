INVESTOR RELATIONS

Surrounding Comparable Price

We sell our projects at a lower price than market comparables in order to increase sales speed.

Approximate Bank Appraisal

If we are unable to sell the asset and we can go to a bank to get a loan.

Sales Price

Sales revenue is paid to SPV escrow account. Only after the sales company has completed their underwriting contract can they get their profit.

Tax, Transaction, Management Fee Tax+Transaction Fee

Typically 5.5-6% of the underwriting sales price. The underwriting company bears responsibility for tax above the underwriting sales price, project company tax(if any), sales costs and other tax issues. Therefore the SPV's costs are minimal and definable.

Acquisition Cost

Total cost of the project to be paid to the developer. Acquisition cost is different than investment required.

IRR

Net Profit/First Installment Payment. It is not net profit / acquisition cost.

Underwriting Sales Completion Time

Guaranteed sales length by underwriting company. Effectively this is the project length. Also note that cash flow occurs throughout the project. Positive cash flow could occur at the 6-month mark. Therefore IRR is higher than ROI on a 12-month project length.

Underwritten Sales Contract

Guaranteed Price Sales Company promises to cover all transaction, tax, sales expense above the underwritten price. At projects close, the sales company is able to withdraw funds above the underwriting price. Therefore, the investor can understand the underwriting price is the SPV's fixed expected revenue after sales costs and most taxes. The SPV will have a deposit from the underwriting company to enforce the sales performance.

First Installment Payment(Investment Required)

The SPV will have the right to purchase the entire project's property deeds. However, the initial payment would acquire a portion of the property deeds, for example the first floor(best assets) of the project. The acquisition is by asset transfer title of those initial shops. Sales revenue pays for the remaining payments for the entire acquisition. Therefore, although the project might cost 400 million, the investment required might be only 100 million. Leverage greatly increases IRR.
