CHINESE POLICY

China’s 2020 Three Red Line policy limits developers willingness to acquire debt, increases equity investment requirements, and pushes developers to adjust their balance sheets by selling projects enbloc to investors. China’s 2020 Three Red Line policy defines 3 debt metrics and classifies developers based on their compliance. The 3 debt metrics are (1)asset-liability ratio below 70%, (2) net debt ratio below 100%, (3) cash-short-term debt ratio below 1.

Any debt metric a real estate developer or subsidiary surpasses causes the developers rating to be downgraded. Developers can obtain favorable interest rates such as 5%, 10%, 15%, by having a better rating. “Red” developers are not able to access any new lending.

GreenTree, an investment sales operator, benefits from Three Red Line policy. Greentree acquires completed enbloc projects from illiquid developers and strata sells the individual units. GreenTree, an asset management company, is not affected by the Red Line policy.
